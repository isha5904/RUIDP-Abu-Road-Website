Project Overview

About Abu Road

Abu Road is a city in the Sirohi district of Rajasthan, India, situated on the banks of the West Banas river. The city is surrounded by the Aravalli hill range and is located about 27 km from Mount Abu. It's a tehsil headquarters and an important commercial and industrial hub.

The Abu Road Municipality has a population of 55,599 (2011 Census) and administers 11,269 houses providing basic amenities like water and sewerage.

About RUIDP

Rajasthan Urban Infrastructure Development Project (RUIDP) is a government project in Rajasthan focused on improving urban infrastructure with support from Government of India and Asian Development Bank since 1998.

This Phase-IV project in Abu Road includes comprehensive water supply and sewerage systems development.

Water Supply System

Bhaisa Singh Dam

The primary source of drinking water for Abu Road, constructed across a tributary of West Banas River Originating in the Aravalli Range. The dam was built by the Water Resources Department of Rajasthan and completed in 2013-2014. While initially intended for irrigation, it is now a primary source of drinking water for Abu Town.  The dam has:

  • Gross storage capacity: 5.07 MCM
  • Live storage: 4.616 MCM
  • Dead storage: 0.453 MCM

Intake Well & Pumping Station

  • Capacity of intake and pumping house to abstract 10 MLD water
  • Intake well cum pump house (diameter - 10 m height - 12 m)
  • 400mm DI transmission pipeline (Length Near About 11km to WTP)

Water Treatment Plant

  • 10 MLD capacity water treatment plant with arrangements:
  • Processes:
    1. Alum coagulation & flocculation
    2. Sedimentation,
    3. Rapid gravity filtration,
    4. Disinfection with chlorination
    5. Wash water recovery
    6. Sludge drying beds
  • Laboratory, office etc.,
  • Clear water reservoir of 2400 KL capacity
  • Location: Near Moksha Dham on Banas River banks
  • Coordinates: 24°28'28.72"N, 72°45'43.90"E

Community Awareness & Participation Program (CAPP)

The CAPP initiative mobilizes, motivates, and facilitates community participation in the project through awareness campaigns and engagement activities.

Implementation Phases

  1. Preparatory Phase: Baseline surveys and planning
  2. Implementation Phase: Awareness campaigns and activities
  3. Follow-up Phase: Monitoring and evaluation

Activities & Methods

  • Stakeholder awareness meetings
  • Ward-level public consultations
  • Focus group discussions
  • Street plays and puppet shows
  • School competitions and rallies
  • Home visits and handbill distribution
